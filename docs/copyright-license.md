## 1. The Core Question

When you use Seed Studio, who creates the image -- you or the software? This is a question with implications for copyright law, commercial licensing, and intellectual property rights.

### Our Position

Seed Studio is a **tool**, not an autonomous artist. You select the layout, palette, shape, density, scale, rotation, noise, resolution, and seed value -- a set of creative decisions that constitute **human authorship**. The code executes your choices deterministically. Therefore, **the user is the author**, and Seed Studio is the instrument.

This position is consistent with:
- **U.S. Copyright Office guidance** (March 2023): Works containing sufficient human creative input in selection, arrangement, and modification of AI-generated elements are copyrightable
- **UK Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988, Section 9(3)**: Computer-generated works vest authorship in "the person by whom the arrangements necessary for the creation of the work are undertaken"
- **Common industry practice**: Tools like Photoshop, Blender, and TouchDesigner do not claim ownership of user-created work

## 5. Who Owns the Copyright?

### The Short Answer

**You do** -- provided you made creative choices (selected a layout, palette, parameters, seed). The generated image is your creative work, protected by copyright if it contains sufficient human authorship.

### The Legal Analysis

Under current U.S. copyright law (17 U.S.C. Section 102), copyright protects "original works of authorship fixed in any tangible medium of expression." The threshold is low (Feist Publications v. Rural Telephone Service, 1991), requiring only a "modicum of creativity."

Seed Studio provides a parameter space that you navigate through creative choices:
- **Layout selection**: Vortex, Mondrian, Galaxy, Kaleidoscope, or Fluid -- fundamentally different visual languages
- **Palette selection**: 8 curated palettes, each producing radically different moods
- **Shape selection**: 5 shape types or mixed
- **Continuous parameters**: Density (5-100), scale (0.2-3.0), rotation (0-360 degrees), noise (0-1.0) -- infinite combinations
- **Seed selection**: 1 billion possible seeds, each producing a unique arrangement

These choices constitute human authorship. The code executes your creative intent deterministically.

### What If You Generate Randomly?

The "Randomize" button selects all parameters randomly. A court could argue that purely random generation lacks human authorship. However:
- You chose to use the randomize function (a creative decision)
- You chose to keep or discard the result (curatorial selection)
- You likely adjusted parameters afterward (iterative refinement)

If you are concerned about copyright strength, we recommend making at least one intentional parameter adjustment after randomization (e.g., tweak density from 40 to 42). This establishes the "creative spark" that copyright law requires.

### Recommendations for Strong Copyright

1. **Make conscious selections** rather than relying entirely on randomize
2. **Iterate and refine** -- the creative process of adjusting parameters and re-rendering is evidence of authorship
3. **Document your process** -- save your settings JSON files as a record of creative decisions
4. **Register copyright** for commercially valuable images (U.S. Copyright Office allows registration of computer-generated works with sufficient human authorship)

## 7. Edge Cases

### 7.1 Two Users Generate the Same Image

If User A (Pro) and User B (Free) both enter seed `123456789` with identical parameters, they generate identical images. User A has a commercial license; User B has a personal-use-only license. Both own the copyright to their independent creation. User B cannot enforce against User A's commercial use because both arrived at the result independently.

### 7.2 AI Training on Generated Images

You own your generated images and control whether they are used for AI training. Seed Studio does not use generated images for model training. However, if you publicly post a generated image (e.g., on social media), third-party AI training scrapers may include it in training datasets. This is outside Seed Studio's control.

### 7.3 Trademark in Generated Images

If you use a generated image as a logo or brand identifier, you may be able to claim trademark rights in that specific use. Seed Studio does not claim any trademark rights in your generated images. However, the generated image's distinctiveness must come from your use in commerce -- the image alone is copyright, not trademark.

### 7.4 Generated Images That Resemble Existing Works

With 1 billion seeds and infinite parameter combinations, some outputs may coincidentally resemble existing artworks or designs. Seed Studio makes no warranty of non-infringement. You are responsible for checking that your generated image does not infringe on existing copyrights or trademarks before commercial use. We recommend reverse image search (Google Images, TinEye) for commercially valuable outputs.

### 7.5 Free User Upgrades to Pro Mid-Session

If you generate images as a Free user and then upgrade to Pro, the images generated before upgrade remain under the Free license (personal use only, watermarked). Images generated after upgrade are covered by the Pro license.

### 7.6 Collaboration and Shared Seeds

If you share a seed with another user and you both generate the same image, you each hold independent copyright and license rights based on your individual Pro/Free tier status.
